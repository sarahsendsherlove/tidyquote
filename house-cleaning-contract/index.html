House Cleaning Contract Template

A free house cleaning contract (service agreement) you can copy, paste and customize — plus a plain-English breakdown of every clause your cleaning business should include so you look professional, get paid on time, and avoid disputes. No sign-up.

A written agreement is the single fastest way for a solo cleaner to look legitimate and prevent the two most common problems: scope creep ("can you also do the garage?") and late or disputed payment. It doesn't need to be long — it needs to be clear. Here's exactly what to include.

What a cleaning service agreement should include

  1. The parties & property. Your business name and the client's name, plus the service address. This is who's bound and where the work happens.
  2. Scope of work. The single most important clause. List exactly what's included (rooms, tasks) and, just as importantly, what's not (windows, laundry, inside the oven) so add-ons are priced, not assumed.
  3. Schedule & frequency. One-time, weekly, biweekly or monthly; the day/time window; and your access method (key, lockbox, someone home).
  4. Price & payment terms. The rate per visit, what triggers a higher rate (first deep clean, extra add-ons), accepted payment methods, and when payment is due (e.g. on completion).
  5. Cancellation & lockout policy. How much notice you require and the fee for a late cancel or a no-access visit. This protects the income you blocked off for them.
  6. Supplies & equipment. Who provides cleaning products and equipment, and any products the client wants you to use or avoid (allergies, surfaces, pets).
  7. Satisfaction / re-clean guarantee. A simple "tell us within 24 hours and we'll fix the spot" clause builds trust and heads off chargebacks.
  8. Liability & breakage. A reasonable limit on liability, how breakage is handled, and a note that you carry insurance if you do.
  9. Term & termination. That either side can end recurring service with reasonable notice (e.g. 7 days), in writing.
  10. Signatures & date. Both parties sign and date. A text or email "I agree" can work for small jobs, but a signature is cleaner.

Replace anything in [brackets]. This is a plain starting point — adapt it to your services and check your local requirements.

HOUSE CLEANING SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Agreement is made on [DATE] between:
  Cleaner / Company: [YOUR BUSINESS NAME], "the Cleaner"
  Client: [CLIENT NAME], "the Client"
  Service address: [SERVICE ADDRESS]

1. SCOPE OF WORK
   The Cleaner will provide the following at each visit:
     [e.g. kitchen, bathrooms, dusting, vacuuming, mopping, trash]
   Not included unless agreed in writing and priced separately:
     [e.g. interior windows, laundry, inside oven/fridge, walls]

2. SCHEDULE
   Frequency: [one-time / weekly / biweekly / monthly]
   Day & time window: [e.g. Tuesdays, 9am-12pm]
   Access: [key / lockbox code / client present]

3. PRICE & PAYMENT
   Rate: $[AMOUNT] per visit.
   First/deep clean rate (if applicable): $[AMOUNT].
   Payment due: [on completion / within X days] by [methods].
   Late payment after [X days] may incur a fee of [$/%].

4. CANCELLATION & LOCKOUT
   Please give at least [24/48] hours' notice to cancel or
   reschedule. Late cancellations or no-access visits may be
   charged [$AMOUNT or % of the visit].

5. SUPPLIES
   [Cleaner provides all products and equipment / Client provides ___].
   Products to avoid (allergies, surfaces, pets): [____].

6. SATISFACTION GUARANTEE
   If anything is missed, notify the Cleaner within 24 hours and the
   affected area will be re-cleaned at no charge.

7. LIABILITY
   The Cleaner will take reasonable care. Please secure or point out
   fragile/high-value items in advance. Liability is limited to
   [the cost of the visit / $AMOUNT]. The Cleaner [does/does not]
   carry liability insurance.

8. TERM & TERMINATION
   Either party may end recurring service with [7] days' written
   notice. Outstanding balances remain due.

Signed:
  Cleaner: ______________________  Date: __________
  Client:  ______________________  Date: __________

Do I really need a contract for house cleaning?

For a one-off clean, a clear text or email confirming scope, price and date is often enough. The moment you take on recurring clients, a short written agreement pays for itself: it sets expectations, justifies your cancellation fee, and gives you something to point to when a client asks for "just one more thing." It also makes you look like a real business — which lets you charge more.

Should I charge a deposit?

For first-time deep cleans and move-out jobs (your most expensive services), many cleaners take a 25–50% deposit or require payment on completion before leaving. For trusted recurring clients, payment on the day or weekly invoicing is normal. Whatever you choose, write it into the payment clause so there's no ambiguity.

How is this different from an invoice?

The contract sets the terms before work starts; the invoice requests payment after a visit. You want both — the agreement prevents disputes, the invoice gets you paid and keeps clean records. Can I use this template commercially?

Yes — copy it, adapt it, and use it with your own clients. It's a starting skeleton, not legal advice; for commercial or high-value contracts, have a local professional review your final version.

Does it need to be signed on paper?

No. A typed name and date, or a clear "I agree to these terms" by email or text, is generally enough for routine residential cleaning. A signature line just makes it tidier and is worth it for recurring or higher-value work.
