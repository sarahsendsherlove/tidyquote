Move-Out Cleaning Checklist

A complete, room-by-room move-out cleaning checklist you can print or tick off on your phone. Built for cleaning-business owners, tenants protecting a deposit, and landlords turning a unit. Free, no sign-up.

A move-out clean (also called an end-of-tenancy or move-in clean) is the most thorough clean there is, because the home is empty and every surface is in play — inside cabinets and closets, behind appliances, interior windows, walls top-to-bottom. It's usually your highest-priced service and often gets inspected by a landlord or property manager, so use this list so nothing gets missed.

Kitchen

  • Empty & wipe inside all cabinets, drawers and pantry shelves
  • Clean inside, outside & under the oven, stovetop and range hood
  • Empty, defrost if needed, and wipe inside the fridge & freezer
  • Pull out fridge/oven and clean the floor & wall behind (if movable)
  • Clean dishwasher inside, filter and door seal
  • Clean microwave inside and out
  • Scrub sink & faucet; descale hard-water spots; clear the drain
  • Wipe counters, backsplash and all cabinet fronts & handles
  • Clean light fixtures, switch plates and any vents
  • Sweep and mop the floor including edges, corners & under units

Bathrooms

  • Scrub & disinfect toilet — bowl, seat, lid, base and behind
  • Deep-scrub shower/tub: remove all soap scum & hard-water film
  • Scrub grout & tile; clean shower glass, door and tracks
  • Empty & wipe inside vanity, cabinets and drawers
  • Clean sink, counter & drain; shine mirror and all fixtures
  • Wipe baseboards, door, frame and light switches
  • Clean exhaust fan cover and any vents
  • Empty trash; sweep and mop the floor

Bedrooms, Closets & Living Areas

  • Empty & wipe inside all closets, shelves and built-ins
  • Dust and wipe ceiling fans, light fixtures and vents
  • Clean interior windows, sills, ledges and tracks
  • Dust and wipe blinds, shutters or window coverings
  • Wipe all baseboards, doors, frames, handles & switch plates
  • Spot-clean walls — scuffs, marks & fingerprints
  • Remove any nails/hooks and fill small holes (if agreed)
  • Vacuum carpets, rugs, edges & closet floors
  • Sweep and mop all hard floors

Whole-Home & Final Walk-Through

  • Cobweb check — every corner, ceiling & door frame
  • Wipe every high-touch point: handles, rails, switches, thermostat
  • All baseboards, doors and frames throughout the home
  • All interior windows, tracks and sills throughout
  • Clean air vents, returns and replace/clean filter (if included)
  • Laundry area: wipe washer/dryer surfaces and lint trap
  • Sweep garage, patio, balcony or entryway (if included)
  • Remove all trash from the property
  • Final walk-through; take before/after photos (with permission)

How much should a move-out clean cost?

Because every surface is in play and the home is empty, a move-out clean is usually the priciest service a cleaner offers — commonly 1.5–2.5× a standard clean for the same square footage. Heavily soiled units, pet homes, or post-renovation jobs run higher. Rather than guessing, price the specific job — square footage, condition and add-ons — with the free House Cleaning Price Calculator, then put the number in writing before you start.

Move-out clean vs. deep clean — what's the difference?

A deep clean happens in a furnished home and tackles built-up grime a weekly clean never reaches. A move-out clean happens in an empty home, so it adds everything that's normally blocked: inside all cabinets, closets and drawers, behind and under appliances, interior windows and tracks, and walls top to bottom. Because a landlord may inspect it against the deposit, it's held to the highest standard.

How do tenants get their full deposit back?

Most deposit disputes come down to cleaning. Work top-to-bottom so dust falls onto surfaces you clean later, don't forget the easy-to-miss spots (inside the oven, behind the fridge, window tracks, baseboards), and photograph the finished home the day you hand back the keys. If you're hiring a cleaner, ask for a written scope and an itemized receipt — that paperwork is exactly what protects both sides.

Can I print this checklist?

Yes. Tap Print / Save as PDF at the top — it's formatted to print cleanly in black and white. Many cleaners print one per job, or laminate a copy and tick it off with a dry-erase marker.

Is a move-out clean the same as a move-in clean?

The task list is essentially identical — both happen in an empty home. The only difference is timing and who's paying: a move-out clean is done by the departing tenant (or their cleaner) to protect a deposit; a move-in clean is done for the arriving occupant who wants a fresh start. Use this same checklist for either.

What's usually NOT included?

Unless agreed up front, move-out cleans typically exclude exterior windows, carpet shampooing/steam cleaning, wall repainting, and hauling away large junk or furniture. Spell out inclusions and exclusions in a short written agreement so there's no dispute at inspection.
