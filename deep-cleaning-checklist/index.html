Deep Cleaning Checklist

A complete, room-by-room deep cleaning checklist you can print or tick off on your phone. Built for cleaning-business owners and anyone tackling a serious top-to-bottom clean. Free, no sign-up.

A deep clean goes beyond surface tidying — it tackles the build-up a weekly clean never reaches: inside appliances, grout, baseboards, behind and under things. It's typically the first visit with a new client and costs roughly 1.5–2× a standard clean. Use this list so nothing gets missed and your quality stays consistent.

Kitchen

  • Wipe counters, backsplash & shine the sink and faucet
  • Clean inside & around the oven, including the door glass
  • Empty, wipe and deodorize inside the fridge & freezer
  • Degrease the range hood, filter & backsplash
  • Clean microwave inside and out
  • Wipe cabinet fronts & handles; spot-clean drips
  • Descale faucet, remove hard-water spots
  • Wipe small appliances (toaster, kettle, coffee maker)
  • Sweep, then mop the floor including edges & corners
  • Empty trash, wash the bin, fresh liner

Bathrooms

  • Scrub & disinfect toilet — bowl, seat, lid, base and behind
  • Deep-scrub shower/tub: remove soap scum & hard-water film
  • Scrub grout & tile detail; clean shower glass and tracks
  • Clean sink, counter & drain; shine mirror and fixtures
  • Wipe cabinet fronts; clean inside if requested
  • Wipe baseboards, door & light switches
  • Dust/clean exhaust fan cover
  • Empty trash, restock paper, mop floor

Bedrooms & Living Areas

  • Dust all surfaces, furniture, electronics & decor
  • Dust ceiling fans, light fixtures & vents
  • Dust baseboards, window sills & ledges
  • Wipe doors, frames, handles & light switches
  • Clean mirrors & interior glass
  • Spot-clean walls, marks & fingerprints
  • Make beds / change linens if requested
  • Vacuum carpets, rugs, edges & under cushions
  • Sweep & mop all hard floors
  • Empty all trash bins

Whole-Home & Finishing Touches

  • Cobweb check — corners, ceilings & door frames
  • Wipe every high-touch point: handles, rails, switches, remotes
  • All baseboards throughout the home
  • All interior doors & frames
  • Laundry / dryer area surfaces
  • Final walk-through; take before/after photos (with permission)

How long does a deep clean take?

A thorough deep clean of an average 1,500 sq ft home usually takes one cleaner 4–7 hours, or a team of two roughly 2–4 hours — significantly longer than a standard maintenance clean. Heavily soiled homes, post-renovation dust, or move-out jobs can run longer. Always quote a deep clean as your highest rate and give yourself buffer; underestimating the first visit is the most common way new cleaners lose money.

Deep clean vs. standard clean — what's the difference?

A standard clean maintains an already-cared-for home: surfaces, floors, bathrooms, kitchen, trash. A deep clean adds everything that builds up over time — inside the oven and fridge, grout and soap scum, baseboards, ceiling fans, doors and frames, behind and under movable items. Most cleaning businesses require a deep clean for the first visit, then switch the client to standard recurring service.

What should you charge for a deep clean?

Because it takes longer and is more physical, a deep clean typically runs 1.5–2× a standard clean for the same home. Rather than guessing, price the specific job — square footage, type of clean, and add-ons — with the free House Cleaning Price Calculator, then adjust to your local market.

Is a deep clean the same as a move-out clean?

Close, but a move-out (or move-in) clean goes further: the home is empty, so you also clean inside all cabinets, closets and drawers, interior windows and tracks, and walls top-to-bottom. It's usually the priciest service because every surface is in play and a landlord may inspect it.
